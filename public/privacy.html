Privacy Policy

Effective Date: July 12, 2026

This Privacy Policy outlines how your data is collected, stored, and managed when using the Sadhana Journal application. We are committed to protecting your privacy in accordance with global standards, including the European Union's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

1. Data Controller and Contact Information

Data Controller: Dhruv Varachhiya

For any questions or to exercise your privacy rights, please contact the Data Controller at the email address provided above.

2. Information We Collect

A. Personal Identification Data

  • Email Address & Password: Managed securely via Google Firebase Authentication. Your password is fully encrypted on Google's servers and is never visible to us.
  • Profile Username: An optional nickname used to customize greetings.

B. Special Category Personal Data (GDPR Article 9)

  • Sadhana & Journal Logs: Dates, completed practices, count values, and optional journal reflection notes. Because spiritual or religious logging may reveal your religious or philosophical beliefs, this information is classified as Special Category Data under GDPR Article 9. We process this data only with your explicit consent.

C. Client-Side Browser Storage (Local Storage)

The app uses local storage on your web browser to store settings and logs. If you do not sign in (Guest Mode), data remains 100% locally on your browser. If you sign in, local storage is used to cache your cloud database. To ensure privacy on shared devices, logging out of your account will completely clear the local storage cache from the device.

3. Lawful Basis for Processing (GDPR Article 6 & 9)

We process your data under the following lawful bases:

  • Explicit Consent (Article 6(1)(a) & Article 9(2)(a)): Given by you voluntarily configuring practices, logging counts, and writing reflections. You may withdraw this consent at any time by deleting your entries, your practices, or your account.
  • Performance of Contract (Article 6(1)(b)): Necessary to sync and back up your journal data to the cloud for authenticated accounts.
  • Legitimate Interests (Article 6(1)(f)): Processing network metadata for security and stability.

4. Sub-Processors and Data Infrastructure

We use the following sub-processors to host and run our infrastructure:

  1. Google LLC (Firebase): Hosts our Authentication and Cloud Firestore database.
  2. Vercel Inc.: Hosts and serves the user interface.

Infrastructure Log Disclosure (IP Addresses)

While the Sadhana Journal app code itself does not collect or store your IP address, our infrastructure sub-processors (Vercel and Google Firebase) automatically log standard network metadata (such as IP addresses, request headers, user-agents, and timestamps) at the server level for security, rate-limiting, DDoS mitigation, and system debugging.

5. International Data Transfers

Google Cloud/Firebase and Vercel utilize global data centers, which may result in your data being stored or processed outside the European Economic Area (EEA), including in the United States. To safeguard these transfers, our sub-processors comply with Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission, ensuring your data receives an equivalent level of protection.

6. Data Retention Period

  • Guest Users: Data remains on your device until you clear your browser cache or delete the site data.
  • Authenticated Users: Account details and logs are retained for as long as you maintain your account. All data is wiped from the database within 30 days of account deletion.

7. Your Privacy Rights

Under the GDPR, you have the following rights:

  • Right of Access & Portability: View all your logged entries directly on your dashboard.
  • Right to Rectification: Edit your logs, counts, and display names in settings.
  • Right to Erasure: Request permanent deletion of your account and all associated cloud data.
  • Right to Lodge a Complaint: You have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority (Data Protection Authority) in your country of residence if you believe your data has been handled unlawfully.

8. Age Limits / Minors Policy

This application is not intended for use by minors. You must be at least 16 years of age (or the minimum legal age of consent in your country of residence) to register an account and sync data to the cloud. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 13.

9. Future Updates and Paid Features

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. If we introduce premium paid features or changes in how data is processed, we will update the effective date at the top of this policy and notify users within the app.
